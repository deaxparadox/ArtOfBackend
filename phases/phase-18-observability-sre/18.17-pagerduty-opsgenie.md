## 1. What Are On-Call Platforms? (Theory)

On-call platforms are the coordination layer between your monitoring/alerting system and your engineers. When Prometheus fires an alert, it doesn't know who is on-call this week, whether to call someone's phone, or how to escalate if they don't respond. PagerDuty and OpsGenie solve this.

```
THE ALERT DELIVERY PROBLEM:

Without an on-call platform:
  Prometheus alert → email to team@company.com
  
  Problems:
  - Email doesn't wake someone up at 3am
  - Who's responsible right now? Everyone and no one
  - If person A doesn't respond, who takes over?
  - How do you know the alert was acknowledged?
  - How do you track incident history?

With PagerDuty/OpsGenie:
  Prometheus alert → on-call platform
  Platform knows: Alice is on-call this week
  Platform delivers: push notification → phone call if no response
  If Alice doesn't acknowledge in 5 minutes: escalate to Bob
  Alice acknowledges: incident log records response time
  
  Clear ownership. Escalation. Accountability. History.
```

## 2. PagerDuty

PagerDuty (2009) is the most widely used commercial incident response platform. It's the de facto standard at large tech companies.

```
PAGERDUTY CORE CONCEPTS:

SERVICES:
  Logical groupings for related alerts.
  "Payment Service", "Database", "API Gateway"
  Each service has its own escalation policy.

ESCALATION POLICIES:
  Rules for who to notify and when.
  
  Example: Payment Service Escalation
    Level 1: On-call engineer (immediate)
             → if no ack in 5 min: call their phone
    Level 2: Senior engineer (10 min of no response)
             → if no ack in 10 more min: call their phone
    Level 3: Engineering manager (20 min of no response)
             → SMS and email

SCHEDULES:
  Who is on-call when.
  
  Example: Weekly rotation
    Week 1: Alice
    Week 2: Bob
    Week 3: Carol
    Week 4: Dave
    
  PagerDuty handles timezone awareness, coverage gaps, override management.

INCIDENTS:
  A triggered alert creates an incident.
  States: triggered → acknowledged → resolved
  Tracks: response time, total duration, responder
  
NOTIFICATION CHANNELS:
  Push notification (PagerDuty mobile app)
  SMS
  Phone call (with voice message reading the alert)
  Email
  Slack (via integration)
```

## 3. Connecting Prometheus to PagerDuty

```yaml
# alertmanager.yml — Prometheus Alertmanager configuration

global:
  resolve_timeout: 5m

route:
  group_by: ['alertname', 'severity']
  group_wait: 30s
  group_interval: 5m
  repeat_interval: 4h
  receiver: 'pagerduty-critical'
  
  routes:
    # SEV1: critical alerts → PagerDuty (wakes people up)
    - match:
        severity: critical
      receiver: 'pagerduty-critical'
    
    # SEV2: warning alerts → Slack (visible during business hours)
    - match:
        severity: warning
      receiver: 'slack-warnings'

receivers:
  - name: 'pagerduty-critical'
    pagerduty_configs:
      - routing_key: '<your-pagerduty-integration-key>'
        description: '{{ .GroupLabels.alertname }}: {{ .CommonAnnotations.summary }}'
        details:
          service: '{{ .CommonLabels.service }}'
          environment: 'production'
        links:
          - href: '{{ .CommonAnnotations.runbook_url }}'
            text: 'Runbook'
          - href: 'https://grafana.yourcompany.com/d/service-overview'
            text: 'Dashboard'

  - name: 'slack-warnings'
    slack_configs:
      - api_url: 'https://hooks.slack.com/services/...'
        channel: '#alerts'
        title: '{{ .GroupLabels.alertname }}'
        text: '{{ .CommonAnnotations.description }}'
        color: '{{ if eq .Status "firing" }}warning{{ else }}good{{ end }}'
```

## 4. OpsGenie

OpsGenie (Atlassian, acquired 2018) is PagerDuty's main competitor. More affordable, slightly simpler, deeply integrated with Jira and other Atlassian tools.

```
OPSGENIE vs. PAGERDUTY:

                    PAGERDUTY       OPSGENIE
────────────────────────────────────────────
PRICING             Higher ($)      Lower ($)
INTEGRATIONS        Most            Good (Atlassian tools excellent)
UI COMPLEXITY       More features   Simpler
ENTERPRISE FEATURES Excellent       Good
REPORTING           Excellent       Good
MOBILE APP          Excellent       Good

CHOOSE PAGERDUTY WHEN:
  ✓ Large enterprise, need advanced features
  ✓ Complex on-call schedules with many teams
  ✓ Need the most comprehensive reporting
  ✓ Already standardized on it

CHOOSE OPSGENIE WHEN:
  ✓ Budget-sensitive
  ✓ Already using Jira/Confluence/Atlassian stack
  ✓ Smaller team, simpler requirements
  ✓ Want comparable features at lower cost

FREE ALTERNATIVES:
  Grafana OnCall (open source, built into Grafana)
  Better Uptime (simple, affordable)
  For very small teams: Slack + cron monitoring may suffice
```

## 5. Incident Management in Practice

```
INCIDENT WORKFLOW WITH PAGERDUTY:

1. Alert fires in Prometheus
2. Alertmanager routes to PagerDuty
3. PagerDuty creates incident, notifies on-call (Alice)
4. Alice acknowledges in PagerDuty app (within 5 min)
5. Alice posts in #incident-2026-06-15 Slack channel
6. Alice investigates and resolves the issue
7. Alice marks incident resolved in PagerDuty
8. PagerDuty records: response time, total duration
9. Monthly report: incidents per week, average response time

KEY METRICS PAGERDUTY TRACKS:
  MTTD: Mean Time to Detect (alert firing to acknowledgment)
    Target: < 5 minutes
  MTTR: Mean Time to Resolve (acknowledge to resolved)
    Target: < 30 minutes for SEV1
  Alert volume per week: high volume = poor alert hygiene
  
USE THESE METRICS TO:
  - Identify engineers who need runbook improvements
  - Identify alerts that consistently take too long to resolve
  - Measure improvement after process changes
```

## 6. Senior Engineer Perspective

**The best pager is one that doesn't ring:**

The quality of your on-call practice is inversely proportional to how often the pager fires. A team with one page per week per engineer is healthy. A team with 20 pages per night has a systemic problem that no amount of PagerDuty configuration will fix.

Every false positive alert is engineer trust eroded. After enough false positives, engineers start ignoring alerts. This is called "alert fatigue" and it's how real incidents get missed. Ruthlessly tune your alerts: if it fired and nothing required action, either fix it or delete it.

**On-call rotation size is a hiring/retention issue:**

Teams with fewer than 4 engineers on rotation burn out their people. On-call every 2 weeks, waking up at 3am regularly, is unsustainable. When building a team, factor on-call sustainability into headcount decisions. "We can't hire because budget" should be balanced against "we're burning out our existing engineers."
